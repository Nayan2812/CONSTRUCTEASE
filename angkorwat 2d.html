Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, located in Cambodia, is the largest religious monument in the world and a symbol of the country's rich cultural heritage. Originally constructed in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, it later transformed into a Buddhist temple. The complex showcases classical Khmer architecture with its grand towers, intricate carvings, and expansive galleries.

Surrounded by a vast moat and lush greenery, Angkor Wat is renowned for its stunning sunrise views, when the temple is beautifully reflected in the nearby water. The monument's walls are adorned with detailed bas-reliefs depicting scenes from Hindu epics and Khmer history. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1992, Angkor Wat attracts millions of visitors every year, standing as a testament to Cambodia's architectural brilliance and spiritual legacy.
