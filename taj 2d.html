Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, situated on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, India, is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love. Commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it took about 22 years to complete, with the efforts of over 20,000 artisans from India and abroad. The monument combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles, featuring a grand white marble dome, intricate carvings, delicate inlay work with precious stones, and beautiful calligraphy. Surrounded by lush Mughal gardens and reflecting pools, the Taj Mahal creates a breathtaking view, especially at sunrise and under the full moon when the marble shimmers in different hues. The complex includes a mosque, a guest house, and a large courtyard. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors from around the world and stands as a testament to India's rich history and cultural heritage.
